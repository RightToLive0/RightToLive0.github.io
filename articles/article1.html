The U.S. Housing Crisis Explained

By RightToLive0

The housing crisis in the United States has been growing for decades, driven by a mix of profit-driven development, lack of affordable options, and political inaction.

In cities like New York, working-class people are being priced out of their own neighborhoods. Rents rise, wages stay flat, and public housing is neglected.

This article dives into why housing is a human right, and how we can push for real change through policy and grassroots activism.
